# General Models and Agent-Based Model Papers

## "Global Sensitivity Analysis: The Primer"
[Link](https://www.andreasaltelli.eu/file/repository/A_Saltelli_Marco_Ratto_Terry_Andres_Francesca_Campolongo_Jessica_Cariboni_Debora_Gatelli_Michaela_Saisana_Stefano_Tarantola_Global_Sensitivity_Analysis_The_Primer_Wiley_Interscience_2008_.pdf)
Authors: A. Saltelli, M. Ratto, T. Andres, F. Campolongo, J. Cariboni, D. Gatelli, M. Saisana, S. Tarantola

**Initial Notes:** *This is an 'easier to read' manual for students like me to understand sensitivity analysis, in order to help us create better models and make the models more defensible. Sensitivity Analysis is the study of how uncertainty in the output of a model can be apportioned to different sources of uncertainty in the model input. And Uncertainty Analysis is quantifying uncertainty in model output. You should start with Uncertainty Analysis, and then do Sensitivity Analysis, they go hand-in-hand. Local sensitivity analysis measures how outputs change when a single point in the input space is changed, while global sensitivity analysis varies the inputs across the entire possible range in order to determine how each input contributes to the output variance overall.*

## "The Strategy of Model Building in Population Biology"
[Link](https://www.jstor.org/stable/27836590?seq=1)
Authors: Richard Levins

**Initial Notes:** *This paper focus's on population biology, but it has some important general ideas of how modeling is limited, and how there are trade-offs between generality, realism, and precision. Often you can get two of them at the loss of one.*
