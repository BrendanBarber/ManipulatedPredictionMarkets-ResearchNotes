# Key Ideas and Definitions

## Bid
An unfilled buy order.

## Ask
An unfilled sell order.

## Order Book
The list of standing orders in a market. Unfilled bid (buy orders) on one side, unfilled asks (sell orders) on the other. It represents what every participant in the market is willing to do, ranked by the price. A trade occurs when an incoming order meets an existing on on the opposite side.

## Marginal Trader (asker/bidder)
The trader currently at the top of the order book (the holder of the best bid or best ask) They are at the boundaring of transacting, so the market's current price reflects their valuation, no the average particpant's.

The "wisdom of crowds" does not directly decide the price, its not "the average of everyone's believed price is 30%", but rather the marginal trader decides the price. But, the crowd's wisdom indirectly shapes the price because the order book determines who ends up at the margin.

## The spread
The gap between the best bid and the best ask. The highest price any buyer will pay versus the lowest price any seller will accept. The width of the spread is a rough indicator of market quality as a tight spread means buyers and sellers are converged and the price is trustworthy, while a wide spread means their is disagreement and/or thin participation.

## Value Trader
A trader who acts on what they perceive as mispricings. They form a probability estimate of the outcome, compare it to the market price, and trade only when there's a gap. They are trading the price not the outcome.

## Outcome Trader
A trader who acts on a belief about what will happen, largely ignoring whether the current price is fair.
